QUANTUM

MECHANICS

Quantum mechanics is the branch of physics that studies the behaviour of matter and energy at the atomic scale, which differs from the rules of classical physics that govern everyday objects. Quantum mechanics reveals a world where particles can exist in multiple states at once, move through barriers, and be connected across vast distances through entanglement.

This fascinating field forms the foundation of modern technology. It challenges our understanding of reality and continues to inspire new discoveries about the universe's most fundamental nature. To learn more, take a deep dive into each page.

SUPERPOSITION Particles exist in multiple states simultaneously until observed collapsing into one. DOUBLE SLIT Shows particles behave like waves and collapse upon observation. ENTANGLEMENT Two particles remain connected so that the state of one instantly affects the other.
