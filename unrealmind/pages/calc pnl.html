The energy \(E\) of a photon is proportional to its frequency \(\nu\):

$$ E = h\nu = \frac{hc}{\lambda} $$

Where:

  • \(h\) = Planck's constant (\(6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}\))
  • \(\lambda\) = Wavelength of light
  • \(c\) = Speed of light (\(3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}\))
  • you also find every time you get a hit here,there's a certain energy deposited here and the energy deposited here happens to be ℏw where w is 2πf
    E=ℏw

The momentum \(p\) of a photon is related to its wavelength:

$$ p = \frac{h}{\lambda} = \frac{E}{c} $$

Where:

  • \(p\) = Momentum of the photon
  • \(h\) = Planck's constant
  • \(\lambda\) = Wavelength of light
  • \(c\) = Speed of light
